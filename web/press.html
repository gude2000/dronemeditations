One paragraph

Drone Meditations is a four-voice synthesizer for the slow, sustained music of meditation, sound healing, and ambient listening — and a serious instrument for anyone who loves drones. Every voice carries its own oscillator (sine, triangle, sawtooth, square, white / pink noise, granular pink noise, or a loaded audio sample), state-variable filter, four LFOs, FM, stereo chorus, mono / stereo / ping-pong delay, and Schroeder reverb. Every LFO can drive multiple destinations at once — pan, amplitude, cutoff, resonance, pitch, FM index — so a single triangle wave can sweep the whole texture in lock-step. Eighty curated presets include tributes to Pauline Oliveros, Éliane Radigue, Stars of the Lid, Sunn O))), William Basinski, Phill Niblock, Harold Budd, Alice Coltrane, Charlemagne Palestine, Keiji Haino, Earth, and Nurse With Wound. Recordings export as 24-bit WAV plus AAC sidecar, DAW-ready.

What makes it different

Multi-target LFOs

One LFO can drive pan + cutoff + pitch + Q + FM + amplitude simultaneously. Six destinations per LFO, tap to toggle. Sixteen modulators across four voices. Rare even in desktop synths.

Tune to Room

Hold a singing bowl, hum a note, strike a tuning fork. The app's pitch detector hears it through the microphone and snaps the chord generator to that exact frequency so the drone never beats against your acoustic source.

Physically-calibrated cymatics

The Chladni-plate renderer is fit from 17 frames of brusspup demo footage, mapping live frequencies to real eigenmode pairs (m,n) on a thin square plate via f ≈ K·(m²+n²). Vibrato breathes between physical modes.

Granular noise synthesis

Pink noise chopped into Hann-windowed grains with controllable size, density, jitter, and pan spread. Geiger-counter ticks at sparse density, rainfall at medium, downpour at high. Negative space is part of the texture.

Quantize to scale

Per-voice toggle that snaps the modulated pitch to your current chord, turning continuous vibrato into chord-tone arpeggios. Faster LFOs become bouncing arpeggios; everything stays in tune.

Ten tuning systems

12-TET, just intonation, Pythagorean, Wendy Carlos α/β/γ, Lou Harrison, Harry Partch's 43-tone, Solfeggio, 432 Hz Verdi, Schumann resonance. Every chord template snaps to the active tuning.

Eighty curated presets

Tributes to Pauline Oliveros, Éliane Radigue, Stars of the Lid, Sunn O))), William Basinski, Phill Niblock, Harold Budd, Alice Coltrane, Charlemagne Palestine, Keiji Haino, Earth, Nurse With Wound. Plus cymatic, spectral, binaural, and drift-showcase sets.

Meditation journeys

Twenty scripted multi-stage sessions: Sundown, Awakening, Floating, Crystal Cave, Spiral Descent, Tibetan Bowl, more. Each auto-advances through preset stages over a fixed duration, fading gently at the end.

Morph between presets

Pick a From preset and a To preset. Drag the slider to morph manually, or let it auto-morph over 30 seconds to an hour. Voices fade in and out smoothly; LFO shapes swap discretely at midpoint.

Mastered export

Recordings produce both a 24-bit PCM WAV (DAW-ready, bit-perfect) and an AAC M4A sidecar (~10× smaller for sharing). Loudness-normalized to -16 dBFS RMS, with 2-second fade-in / 4-second fade-out and embedded metadata.

About the developer

Drone Meditations is a personal project by Jose Gude, an independent developer and physician. The app started as an exploration of slow music and physically-calibrated cymatic visualization, then grew into a serious synthesizer over six months of design and DSP work. Both the web app (dronemeditations.com, free) and the iOS app are built from the same musical foundation, with the iOS version adding granular synthesis, microphone-based tuning, the mastered WAV / M4A export pipeline, meditation journeys, and the physically-calibrated cymatic visualizer.

"I wanted an instrument that could give a sound healer in a session room the same musical depth a Eurorack player gets in a studio — and that would let a meditator listen with their eyes closed too. The same app for both. No accounts. No subscriptions. Just sound."
